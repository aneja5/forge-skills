# Evaluation Criteria

How to decide whether a branch needs more drilling or is resolved.

## A branch is RESOLVED when the answer is:

- **Specific** — names a user segment, not "everyone"; names a number, not "a lot"
- **Falsifiable** — could be proven wrong with real-world evidence
- **Decided** — not "maybe X or maybe Y"; a clear position was taken

Examples of resolved answers:
- "Freelance designers with 1-5 clients who invoice weekly" (specific segment)
- "We'll charge $29/month, targeting 500 customers in year 1" (specific + falsifiable)
- "We're NOT building a mobile app in v1" (decided, in-scope/out-scope clarity)

## A branch needs MORE DRILLING when the answer:

- Uses vague terms: "people", "businesses", "everyone", "scale", "better UX"
- Assumes without evidence: "everyone has this problem", "they'll pay for it"
- Contradicts something said in an earlier branch
- Answers a different question than the one asked
- Uses hedge words: "probably", "might", "could potentially", "hopefully"

## Branch-specific signals

### Problem
Resolved: specific user type + specific painful moment + frequency estimate
Still vague: "developers are frustrated with slow tools"

### Founder fit
Resolved: concrete credential, lived experience, or unfair network advantage
Still vague: "I'm passionate about this space"

### Solution
Resolved: what the product does + explicit list of what it does NOT do
Still vague: "a platform that helps with X"

### Business model
Resolved: who pays, how much, on what trigger (per seat, per transaction, subscription)
Still vague: "we'll monetize through the data" or "enterprise deals"

### Distribution
Resolved: specific first channel + named person/community/publication to reach through
Still vague: "SEO and word of mouth" or "we'll run ads"

### Risks
Resolved: single most likely failure mode named — not a list
Still vague: "there are some regulatory risks" or listing 5 risks without ranking

### MVP
Resolved: one shippable thing that proves or disproves the riskiest assumption
Still vague: "we'll build a simple version first" or a feature list

## Drilling techniques

When an answer is vague, make the vagueness explicit:

> "You said 'small businesses' — do you mean a bootstrapped 2-person shop or a 50-person company with a finance team?"

> "You said 'better UX' — better than what specific tool, measured how?"

> "You said 'word of mouth' — who specifically are the first 3 people who would tell someone else, and why would they?"
